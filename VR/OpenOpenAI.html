Brief

OpenOpenAI is a puzzle experience that takes the player on a mission to locate the missing pieces of ChatGPT’s circuit board/brain/mind. The player navigates through various locations, including the OpenAI planet, Sam Altman’s room, and Sam’s research lab.

User Journey

The story is set in a futuristic world where several researchers are studying the mind of ChatGPT and are on a mission to locate all the missing pieces from its circuit board. The available interaction includes navigating freely across the scenes and the ability to grab and drop the circuit boards with a controller.

During the game, the player navigates through the various locations in search of the missing pieces. As they find all the pieces of each room, a portal appears in the space, letting them level up and navigate to the next room. When the player collects all the pieces, they have to return to the starting point and assemble them on the puzzle (circuit)board.

Throughout the game, the player is given context and guidance by an AI created ex professo to assist on this mission.

Concept

My role at OpenOpenAI drew from all of the mentioned aspects of my professional profile. Starting with the common interest in working on a puzzle game and drawing from the storyline creation assisted by ChatGPT, I proposed making such a working relationship with ChatGPT as the base of our storyline and gameplay – i.e., creating a VR experience inside a narrative world created by ChatGPT as an exploration of ChatGPT’s mind. Pretty meta.

Storyline + Gameplay

The script for the storyline, developed with Catalina, was a result of iterative re-processing through ChatGPT. We ended up with a highly edited script that’s user-friendly and short enough to be narrated through voiceover without negatively disturbing the player’s experience. That also structured – and was structured by – the gameplay and level design.

Custom code

I contributed various scripts for the implementation of the puzzle pieces collection:

GameEvents.cs

It implements an event system that allows for counting the pieces grabbed and the pieces placed on the puzzle board at the end of the game, as well as for knowing when to activate the portals and when is the game finished.

PPiece.cs

When the puzzle piece is grabbed, it calls the OneMorePiece() function from GameEvents.cs events manager and deactivates the piece after 2 seconds.

PuzzleManager.cs

Prompted by the event system, it counts the puzzle pieces (grabbed and placed). It opens the portals when appropriate and informs the player of the measurements.

Score.cs

It renders the score (pieces grabbed) on the screen for the player.

Sound design

Using samples and synthesized sounds edited in Ableton Live, I made the interaction sounds and the background musical loop. For the final project.

Reflection

Our brief covered an immersive 3D experience of a challenging puzzle, all guided by a compelling narrative. My assessment would be that our team accomplished an immersive 3D experience guided by a compelling narrative. The major improvements to the project could be done on the level design and gameplay to make the puzzle game more challenging – challenging puzzles are challenging to design of course. The interaction works well after overcoming some glitches and dizzying effects, but could still run more smoothly.
