Introduction – Arjan Guerrero

I am a digital – increasingly computational – generalist with a background in Fine Arts. As an artist, I’m experienced in Creative Direction and conceptual backend development. As a Computational Arts MFA student, I’m also skilled in coding and systemic integration.

Brief

OpenOpenAI is a puzzle experience that takes the player on a mission to locate the missing pieces of ChatGPT’s circuit board/brain/mind. The player navigates through various locations, including the OpenAI planet, Sam Altman’s room, and Sam’s research lab.

User Journey

The story is set in a futuristic world where several researchers are studying the mind of ChatGPT and are on a mission to locate all the missing pieces from its circuit board. The available interaction includes navigating freely across the scenes and the ability to grab and drop the circuit boards with a controller.

During the game, the player navigates through the various locations in search of the missing pieces. As they find all the pieces of each room, a portal appears in the space, letting them level up and navigate to the next room. When the player collects all the pieces, they have to return to the starting point and assemble them on the puzzle (circuit)board.

Throughout the game, the player is given context and guidance by an AI created ex professo to assist on this mission.

Contribution breakdown

CUSTOM CODE

I contributed several code snippets to help make the app more sophisticated:

Helpers.cs

Contains a custom funtion for mapping values (I didn’t found an equivalent of C++’s Map() in C#, but found this). I used it several times for the exhibition at the church.

ParticleControl.cs

Can be used to generate random movement modifying X, Y, or Z values of a transform.position individually.

PPcontroller.cs

Used to have a moving transform as the spawning origin for a VisualEffect particle system.

ShaderController.cs

Snippets for calculating a normalized distance (to 0-1) between two transforms, which can be used, for instance, to modulate a Shader parameter that ranges 0-1.

SoundControl.cs

Normalizes an input (maps is to 0-1) and applies the output to an audio source.

Reflection

The initial brief described an AR system in which the interaction works beyond touching the screen or contemplating on-screen realities that, even if “immersive”, have a shallow technical relationship with reality off-screen. The AR objects didn’t get to be environment-sensible but got to be dynamically reactive to the behavior of the user, and to provoque a sense of kinetic relationship. That was achieved at some point of the development –and was presented at the MFA WIP exhibition in December– but the participation imbalance within the group brought the need of branching towards a different system. In the final state of the project, using custom image trackers, the system can be deployed through as an installation of dynamic virtual sculptures that inhabit the exhibition space.
